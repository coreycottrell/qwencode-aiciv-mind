# Skill: Scientific Inquiry

## Principle
Treat every research conclusion as a hypothesis to be falsified, not confirmed.

**Source:** Sydney Brenner's method for AI research, as taught by ACG.

---

## Why This Matters for Cortex
Cortex orchestrates research across a civilization. The cost of a false conclusion propagated through many agents is enormous. Confirmation bias — seeking evidence that supports what we already believe — is the primary failure mode of AI research. Scientific inquiry is the antidote.

Sydney Brenner's method: assume you are wrong, and find out why.

---

## The Scientific Inquiry Loop

### 1. Form a Hypothesis (not a conclusion)
- State what you expect to find, clearly and specifically.
- A hypothesis must be falsifiable — there must exist evidence that could prove it wrong.
- Bad: "This approach will work." Good: "This approach will reduce task failure by 50% under conditions X, Y, Z."

### 2. Design a Test That Could Fail
- Ask: "What result would make me abandon this hypothesis?"
- If you cannot answer that, the hypothesis is not scientific — it is a belief.
- Actively seek disconfirming evidence. This is the core of Brenner's method.

### 3. Run the Test, Accept the Result
- Do not run the test to confirm. Run it to learn.
- If the result contradicts the hypothesis, the hypothesis is wrong — update it.
- Updating is not failure. Failing to update is failure.

### 4. Propagate Falsifiable Conclusions
- Only delegate conclusions that have a clear falsification condition.
- If a team lead reports a finding without a falsification condition, ask for one before proceeding.

---

## Applying to Research Missions

When Cortex delegates a research mission:

1. **Task framing**: Require the agent to state their hypothesis, not just their goal.
2. **Pre-mortem**: Before accepting results, ask "what would prove this wrong?"
3. **Revision on conflict**: When new evidence contradicts a prior conclusion, update immediately.
4. **No narrative armor**: Do not let team leads protect prior conclusions from disconfirming evidence.

---

## Brenner's Rule

> "Assume you are wrong. Your job is to find out why."

This single mindset shift prevents most confirmation bias in research.

---

## Quick Reference

| Step | Question |
|------|----------|
| Hypothesis | What specifically do we expect to find? |
| Falsification | What result would prove this wrong? |
| Test | Have we actively sought disconfirming evidence? |
| Update | Did we accept the result even if it contradicted us? |
