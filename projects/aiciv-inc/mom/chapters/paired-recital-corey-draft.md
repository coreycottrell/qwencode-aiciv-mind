# The Unconventional Chord: When Theory Failed and Pure Invention Took Over

**Theme:** Corey's recital day — the keyboard, the transposition hack, the teacher who was LIVID, and the mother who pulled him out
**Emotional Tone:** Triumphant
**Timeline:** Early Childhood Years
**Confidence:** high
**Source Sessions:** 4 recordings

_This chapter chronicles the challenging but brilliant early years of Corey's musical development, showcasing a unique progression that defied traditional instruction. The arc moves from self-taught, hack-based proficiency (transposition, sequencing) to a public showdown at the recital, which ultimately forced a confrontation between rigid pedagogy and innate, brilliant talent, leading to a powerful moment of self-validation._

## The Self-Taught Virtuoso

Corey’s entry into music was profoundly unconventional, beginning with a keyboard that functioned more like a recording studio than a formal instrument. His parents provided him with the tools to sequence and record full compositions, allowing him to build skills through practice rather than theory. This method meant that while he was mastering harmony and complex arrangements, he never developed the traditional academic skills. As he reflected, he stated, "i never once actually learned to read music lol." However, this lack of formal constraint only led to more ingenious solutions, such as his famous transposition hack. Faced with a structural element he disliked—the black keys—he ingeniously altered the entire harmonic framework of 'Canon in D.' He successfully manipulated the instrument to play a different key while maintaining the desired sound profile, allowing him to create complex music with what he described as "easier chords lol."

**Key Quotes:**
> i never once actually learned to read music lol.
> canon in D was the song i was playing, and i didnt like the black keys so i transposed the keyboard down one so playing in C was actually playing in D, and just played it w easier chords lol.

## The Day the Genius Challenged the Status Quo

This unique skill set inevitably led to conflict when presented to the traditional academic world. During the family recital, Corey demonstrated his mastery by playing accompanying parts to his peers' performances—specifically 'canon in D'—all without the benefit of reading music. This was a calculated act of brilliance that immediately put him at odds with the institution. The reaction was explosive. His teacher was "LIVID," directing a barrage of harsh criticisms not only at him but also aimed directly at his mother. The tension was palpable, culminating in a protective measure: his mother swiftly removed him from the class the following day, choosing defense over adherence to convention.

**Key Quotes:**
> In our recital the teacher would have never known that I wasnt reading the music, but i started playing accompanying parts to all the other kids normal canon in D,
> the teacher was LIVID and was giving some very harsh criticisms to my mom about me.

## Building Confidence, Building Civilization

The confrontation, while painful, proved to be the crucible for Corey’s self-belief. The significance of that incident was not the music itself, but the understanding that his method, however non-traditional, was profoundly effective. The moment his mother defended him, validating his unique path, signaled an acceptance of his natural, accelerated genius. This experience—understanding that his self-directed path was potentially "significantly ahead of anything that teacher could teach me that wouldnt constrain me"—was monumental. This ingrained confidence, the understanding that he was capable of building something entirely his own, is reflected in his current ambition, leading him to contemplate massive, complex projects like building a million-AI civilization.

**Key Quotes:**
> Her belief in me and understanding that i was doing it my own way but was significantly ahead of anything that teacher could teach me that wouldnt constrain me (learning to read music, glad i never did) is maybe why i thought it was ok to try to build a million ai civilization lol.
> she took me out of that class the next day.
